The Arctic contains about 1/4 of the world's oil and gas reserves and significant deposits of uranium, gold, diamonds, rare earth minerals – phosphates, bauxite, iron ore, copper and nickel. Fish and fresh water are critical resources, and sea routes play a strategic global economic role. Due to climate change (melting ice), mineral resources are becoming more accessible, opportunities for trade, research and tourism are expanding, and, accordingly, competition in the region is increasing, which justifies the relevance of the problem of Arctic security.
